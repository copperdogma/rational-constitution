1. AXIOMS (Foundational First Principles)

These are the deep premises that drive the entire constitutional system.

Axiom 1 — Agency

Agents act intentionally over time toward goals.
A system must preserve meaningful agency, not undermine it arbitrarily.

Axiom 2 — Non-Interference

Agents must be able to execute their lawful plans without arbitrary obstruction or unilateral negation of their actions.

Axiom 3 — Predictability

Agents require stable, publicly knowable rules to plan actions and allocate effort.
Systems must minimize arbitrariness and surprise.

Axiom 4 — Moral Equality & Reciprocity

Rules may not assume innate hierarchies.
Any obligation imposed on one agent class must be justifiable under reciprocal conditions.

Axiom 5 — Human Cognitive Bounds

Security and governance rules must be understandable, finite, general, and implementable by human-scale cognition — not requiring omniscience.

2. PROPERTY / PRIVACY FRAMEWORK (Constitutional Clauses)

This is the clean constitutional text.

⸻

I. Action-Result Property Clause

1.1
Agents have exclusive control over physical objects they create or produce through their actions.

1.2
Such control may not be overridden except through voluntary transfer or through enforcement actions that satisfy the requirements of justification and minimal interference established in this Constitution.

⸻

II. Information and Expression Clause

2.1
Agents control their own acts of expression, communication, and disclosure.

2.2
No agent may be compelled to express, disclose, or transmit information except as permitted under the justified enforcement provisions of this Constitution.

2.3
Information voluntarily expressed or communicated to others does not grant the expressing agent control over the internal memory, reasoning, or mental use of that information by recipients.

⸻

III. Natural Resource Allocation Clause

3.1
Unproduced natural resources, including land, minerals, water, and naturally occurring materials, shall be governed by general, publicly knowable rules established by the community.

3.2
Such rules must conform to the principles of equality, reciprocity, predictability, and human comprehensibility set forth in this Constitution.

3.3
No claim of exclusive control over unproduced natural resources may be made except as provided for under these general rules.

⸻

IV. State Action and Individual Targeting Clause (Privacy)

4.1
The state may not take any action directed at an identifiable agent without reasonable grounds to believe that the agent has violated, or is in the process of violating, the law.

4.2
State actions subject to this requirement include, but are not limited to: surveillance, monitoring, tracking, searching, recording, interception, compelled disclosure, detention, and investigative targeting.

4.3
State actions undertaken for general, non-targeted safety or administrative purposes are permitted, provided they apply equally and do not identify or single out specific agents without justification.

⸻

These four clauses form the complete property/privacy foundation of your constitution.

3. FOUNDATIONAL RIGHTS STRUCTURE

(Used for justice system design and implicitly required by your constitution)

This is the rights scaffold your justice/corrections work assumed.

⸻

I. Baseline Rights (Default, Non-Derogable Except by General Law)

These come directly from the axioms; all citizens possess them unless removed by a general law applicable to all, not by punishment alone:
	•	Bodily integrity
	•	Freedom of thought
	•	Freedom from compelled expression except under justified enforcement
	•	Freedom from arbitrary state action
	•	Freedom from arbitrary seizure of created property
	•	Right to general legal equality and reciprocity
	•	Right to predictable, legible rules
	•	Right to due process

These are the “floor.”

⸻

II. Ordinary Civil Rights (Derogable Only by Sentence or General Law)

These can be removed only:
	•	if expressly named in a lawful sentence or
	•	if a general law (applying equally to all citizens) removes them.

These include:
	•	Freedom of movement
	•	Freedom of association
	•	Freedom of communication
	•	Voting rights
	•	Commercial rights (to work, to contract, to buy/sell)
(derogable only via explicit sentencing, not by implication)
	•	Access to public programs (pensions, disability, UBI, etc.)
(derogable only via explicit law or explicit sentence)

⸻

III. Enforcement Rights (Justice System Layer)

These structure the permissible forms of punishment:
	•	Only explicitly named rights may be derogated.
	•	No “implicit” rights-loss just from being imprisoned.
	•	Punishment must be:
	•	proportionate
	•	legible
	•	predictable
	•	minimally invasive beyond the enumerated restrictions

⸻

IV. Retained Rights During Punishment

If movement is restricted (prison-style sentence), all other rights remain unless explicitly derogated:
	•	right to work
	•	right to refuse work
	•	right to spend/keep money
	•	right to pensions/UBI/disability
	•	right to communicate (unless explicitly restricted)
	•	right to bodily autonomy
	•	right to charity and outside support
	•	right to due process within the institution
	•	right to choose self-harming refusals (if competent)

(This structure is the spine of your justice/corrections proposal.)

⸻

V. Capacity & Mental Health Overlay

A cross-cutting system that applies inside and outside penal contexts:
	•	Competent agents may refuse life-sustaining help.
	•	Incapacitated agents trigger the state’s custodial duty of care.
	•	Same rules inside prison and outside.
	•	Determinations require review, documentation, and external oversight.
